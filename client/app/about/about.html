Bible In Your Best Language

About BIYBL

BIYBL - Bible In Your Best Language - helps speakers of other languages engage deeply with God's word during church events.

Many churches are blessed with foreign visitors who do not speak the language the service is conducted in well, if at all. They could be students, refugees, immigrants or simply holidaymakers. These people have been drawn to Christians and their meetings, but how do we help them leave the building with more than just bemusement?

For your church to use BIYBL, sign up on the site. You'll define a username and password that you or your church administrator will use to log in each week to tell BIYBL the passages you are studying that week. You will also be given a well-known URL. This URL can be printed in your church bulletin, turned into a QR code and pasted into your pew Bibles, or set as the home page for the cheap tablets your church hands out on the door to foreign visitors.

That well-known URL simply provides a list of languages to choose from. When the visitor recognises and chooses their language, they are immediately presented with the week's passage(s) in that language. They can then refer to this while the Word is being read and preached, to help them understand better what is going on.

BIYBL is a free service built in October 2015 at the London instance of the Code for the Kingdom Global Hackathon, by Ben Drury, Gervase Markham and David Fedor.
